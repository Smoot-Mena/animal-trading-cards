Leopard

The leopard is the most elusive and secretive of the large felids. They are extremely difficult to trace and locate in the wild.

  • Scientific Name: Panthera pardus
  • Average Length: 90 - 196 cm
  • Average Lifespan: 12 - 17 years
  • Habitat: Sub-Saharan Africa, Savanna and rainforest

Leopards tend to have distinctive dark spots called rosettes, which create beautiful patterns against their otherwise light fur. Black leopards however have dark fur which makes it difficult to see the spots. They appear almost solid black and are often called black panthers.

Red-Tailed Hawk

The red-tailed hawk is capable of “kiting”, holding still against the wind on set wings, much like a kite tugging against string. Its one of the few birds able to do so.

  • Scientific Name: Buteo jamaicensis
  • Average Length: Body: 18 - 26 in; Wingspan: 38 - 43 in
  • Average Lifespan: 21 years
  • Habitat: Open areas with high perching places

Adults are dark brown on the back and the top of their wings. The underside of the bird is usually light with a dark belly band, and a cinnamon wash on the neck and chest.

Bottlenose Dolphin

Dolphins eat fish, squid and crustaceans. They do not chew their food but may break it into smaller pieces before swallowing.

  • Scientific Name: Tursiop
  • Average Length: 4 meters
  • Average Lifespan: 20 - 80 years
  • Habitat: Oceans with warm temperatures

Its color varies considerably, is usually dark grey on the back and lighter grey on the flanks, but it can be bluish-grey, brownish-grey, or even nearly black, and is often darker on the back from the rostrum to behind the dorsal fin.

Honey Badger

For its size, the Honey Badger has an enormous brain. They can undo locks, unwrap wires and climb out of enclosures. They are one of only a few critters on Earth known to use a variety of tools: not only one or two they discovered by accident.

  • Scientific Name: Mellivora capensis
  • Average Length: 22 - 38 in
  • Average Lifespan: 20 - 80 years
  • Habitat: Sub-Saharan African burrows and rock crevices

Honey badgers have long bodies, short legs, broad backs, tiny ear ridges, flatheads, small eyes, short muzzles, and stumpy tails. They have white fur and thick loose skin with a white streak running across their spine.
